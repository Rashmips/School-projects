Background

The purpose of this project is to forecast the final selling price of a chosen house using data obtained from Zillow.com. After registering at Zillow and obtaining a Zillow ID (ZWSID), we are able to stream the neccessary comparables data from the Zillow Web Services API into an R session. This data is then used to build a best regression prediction model. Fitted values and 95% predictional intervals are then compared to the price estimates and ranges, also provided by Zillow.com.

Registering and obtaining a Zillow ID

To create an API account, one must register with Zilllow and obtain a Zillow Web Services ID (ZWSID) at the URL https://www.zillow.com/webservice/Registration.htm . The ZWSID is sent via email, and therefore it is important to check spam folders is one does not receive their ZWSID. The ZWSID is copied and saved into an R vector as character.

Model Assumptions Checking

Now we check for non-constant variance, normality of errors, and influential data.

Conclusion

The model shows positive correlation to amount and lot size and negative correlation to tax assesment value (Essex county has on of the highest property tax rates in the US and property tax is an important consideration here) which appears to be intuitively correct.

There appears to be disagreement between the confidence interval for average sales price and the Zestimate low-high values. It is interesting to note that zillow estimated price and high/low values are very much in agreement with the model prior to removal of influential(high leverage) points.

There is a difinite agreement between price set by the seller of this property and price estimated by our model. The seller is asking 679000 USD which is very slightly lower than our predicted value.

note: I very recently purchased a house in Livingston and have found that, in most cases, the zillow estimate is much lower than the price the house actually sells for.
